𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 Proper Self Introduction and Parts

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 𝗘𝘅𝗽𝗹𝗮𝗻𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻:

Proper self-introduction is the act of presenting yourself to others in a complete and organized way. It helps people understand who you are and builds confidence.

𝗣𝗮𝗿𝘁𝘀 𝗼𝗳 𝗦𝗲𝗹𝗳 𝗜𝗻𝘁𝗿𝗼𝗱𝘂𝗰𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻:

  • 𝗚𝗿𝗲𝗲𝘁𝗶𝗻𝗴: A polite way to start your introduction.
  • 𝗡𝗮𝗺𝗲: Stating your full name.
  • 𝗜𝗻𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗺𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻: Basic details about yourself (age, school, or course).
  • 𝗧𝗿𝗮𝗶𝘁𝘀: Personality or characteristics (e.g., hardworking, friendly).
  • 𝗛𝗼𝗯𝗯𝗶𝗲𝘀: Activities you enjoy doing.
  • 𝗦𝗸𝗶𝗹𝗹𝘀: Talents or abilities you have.
  • 𝗚𝗼𝗮𝗹𝘀: Your plans or dreams for the future.
  • 𝗖𝗹𝗼𝘀𝗶𝗻𝗴: Ending politely, often with thanks.

𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲:

"Hello! I’m Juan. I am hardworking and creative. I enjoy drawing and playing games. My skill is using technology. My goal is to become a successful entrepreneur. Thank you!"

𝗧𝗮𝘀𝗸𝘀
𝗔𝗰𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗶𝘁𝘆 Each student will introduce themselves using the parts of self-introduction: (Greeting, Name, Info, Traits, Hobbies, Skills, Goals, Closing).
𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺 Interview a classmate by asking their name, personality, hobbies, strengths, and goals, and write down your answers.

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 How to Debate

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 𝗘𝘅𝗽𝗹𝗮𝗻𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻:

Debate is a formal discussion where people present their opinions with reasons and evidence. It teaches critical thinking, persuasion, and public speaking.

𝗣𝗮𝗿𝘁𝘀 𝗼𝗳 𝗮 𝗗𝗲𝗯𝗮𝘁𝗲:

  • 𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰: The issue being discussed.
  • 𝗣𝗼𝘀𝗶𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲 𝗦𝗶𝗱𝗲: Supporting the topic.
  • 𝗡𝗲𝗴𝗮𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲 𝗦𝗶𝗱𝗲: Opposing the topic.
  • 𝗔𝗿𝗴𝘂𝗺𝗲𝗻𝘁𝘀: Reasons for your side.
  • 𝗖𝗼𝘂𝗻𝘁𝗲𝗿𝗮𝗿𝗴𝘂𝗺𝗲𝗻𝘁𝘀: Responding to the opposing side’s points.
  • 𝗖𝗼𝗻𝗰𝗹𝘂𝘀𝗶𝗼𝗻: Final summary of your points.

𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲 𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰:

"Should students use mobile phones in school?"
- 𝗣𝗼𝘀𝗶𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲 𝗦𝗶𝗱𝗲: "Mobile phones help students access information quickly."
- 𝗡𝗲𝗴𝗮𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲 𝗦𝗶𝗱𝗲: "Mobile phones distract students from learning."

𝗧𝗮𝘀𝗸𝘀
𝗔𝗰𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗶𝘁𝘆 Teacher finds a topic and divides the class into two groups. Group 1: Negative Side | Group 2: Positive Side. Students will start a debate.
𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺 Search a trending topic and write arguments with evidence.

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 Why Operations are Important

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 𝗘𝘅𝗽𝗹𝗮𝗻𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻:

Math operations are methods used to solve numerical problems. They are essential for everyday calculations, problem-solving, and logical thinking.

𝗣𝗮𝗿𝘁𝘀 𝗼𝗳 𝗠𝗮𝘁𝗵 𝗢𝗽𝗲𝗿𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻𝘀:

  • 𝗔𝗱𝗱𝗶𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻: Combining numbers to get a total.
  • 𝗦𝘂𝗯𝘁𝗿𝗮𝗰𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻: Finding the difference between numbers.
  • 𝗠𝘂𝗹𝘁𝗶𝗽𝗹𝗶𝗰𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻: Repeated addition to calculate totals faster.
  • 𝗗𝗶𝘃𝗶𝘀𝗶𝗼𝗻: Splitting numbers into equal groups.

𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲 𝗣𝗿𝗼𝗯𝗹𝗲𝗺𝘀:

  • 1000 − 419 = 581
  • 1200 + 2973 = 4173
  • 5555 ÷ 55 = 101
  • 6676 × 2733 = 18,236,508
𝗧𝗮𝘀𝗸𝘀
𝗔𝗰𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗶𝘁𝘆 Solve 1000 − 419 and 1200 + 2973 with solution. Write in paper and take a photo.
𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺 Solve 5555 ÷ 55 and 6676 × 2733 with solution. Write in paper and take a photo.

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 How to Create Roleplay and Script

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 𝗘𝘅𝗽𝗹𝗮𝗻𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻:

Roleplay is acting out situations to practice communication, teamwork, and problem-solving. Scripts guide the dialogue and actions of each character.

𝗣𝗮𝗿𝘁𝘀 𝗼𝗳 𝗥𝗼𝗹𝗲𝗽𝗹𝗮𝘆:

  • 𝗦𝗰𝗲𝗻𝗮𝗿𝗶𝗼: The situation or problem being acted out.
  • 𝗖𝗵𝗮𝗿𝗮𝗰𝘁𝗲𝗿𝘀: The people in the scenario.
  • 𝗗𝗶𝗮𝗹𝗼𝗴𝘂𝗲: What each character says.
  • 𝗣𝗹𝗼𝘁 / 𝗙𝗹𝗼𝘄: The order of events from start to finish.
  • 𝗖𝗼𝗻𝗰𝗹𝘂𝘀𝗶𝗼𝗻: How the scenario ends.

𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲:

𝗦𝗰𝗲𝗻𝗮𝗿𝗶𝗼: Working on a group project.
- 𝗖𝗵𝗮𝗿𝗮𝗰𝘁𝗲𝗿 𝗔: "We need to finish this project before class ends."
- 𝗖𝗵𝗮𝗿𝗮𝗰𝘁𝗲𝗿 𝗕: "Let’s divide tasks so everyone can help."
- 𝗖𝗵𝗮𝗿𝗮𝗰𝘁𝗲𝗿 𝗖: "I’ll do research; you prepare the presentation."

𝗧𝗮𝘀𝗸𝘀
𝗔𝗰𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗶𝘁𝘆 Teacher divides the class into two groups and present a roleplay in group chat.
𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺 Create a creative roleplay script.

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 How to Create a Research Title

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 𝗘𝘅𝗽𝗹𝗮𝗻𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻:

A research title is the main focus of a study. It shows what the research is about and guides the entire study.

𝗣𝗮𝗿𝘁𝘀 𝗼𝗳 𝗮 𝗥𝗲𝘀𝗲𝗮𝗿𝗰𝗵 𝗧𝗶𝘁𝗹𝗲:

  • 𝗠𝗮𝗶𝗻 𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰: The central subject of the research.
  • 𝗦𝗰𝗼𝗽𝗲: What the research will cover.
  • 𝗞𝗲𝘆𝘄𝗼𝗿𝗱𝘀: Important words that define the study.
  • 𝗖𝗹𝗮𝗿𝗶𝘁𝘆: Making the title understandable and concise.

𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲𝘀:

  1. "The Effect of Mobile Phones on Students’ Study Habits"
  2. "How Social Media Influences Teen Creativity"
  3. "The Role of School Clubs in Developing Leadership Skills"
𝗧𝗮𝘀𝗸𝘀
𝗔𝗰𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗶𝘁𝘆 Students will create 3 possible research titles about school or student life.
𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺 Choose the best title and explain why it is a good research title.

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 Reading Comprehension

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 𝗘𝘅𝗽𝗹𝗮𝗻𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻:

Reading comprehension is the ability to understand, analyze, and explain a text or story. It helps students identify main ideas, supporting details, and lessons.

𝗣𝗮𝗿𝘁𝘀 𝗼𝗳 𝗥𝗲𝗮𝗱𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗖𝗼𝗺𝗽𝗿𝗲𝗵𝗲𝗻𝘀𝗶𝗼𝗻:

  • 𝗠𝗮𝗶𝗻 𝗜𝗱𝗲𝗮: The central point of the text.
  • 𝗦𝘂𝗽𝗽𝗼𝗿𝘁𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗗𝗲𝘁𝗮𝗶𝗹𝘀: Facts or statements that explain the main idea.
  • 𝗦𝗲𝗾𝘂𝗲𝗻𝗰𝗲 / 𝗢𝗿𝗱𝗲𝗿: How events happen in order.
  • 𝗜𝗻𝗳𝗲𝗿𝗲𝗻𝗰𝗲: Understanding hidden or implied meanings.
  • 𝗟𝗲𝘀𝘀𝗼𝗻 / 𝗠𝗼𝗿𝗮𝗹: The message or learning from the story.

𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲:

𝗦𝘁𝗼𝗿𝘆: A boy learns honesty after trying to cheat.
- 𝗠𝗮𝗶𝗻 𝗜𝗱𝗲𝗮: Honesty is better than cheating.
- 𝗟𝗲𝘀𝘀𝗼𝗻: Being honest builds trust and respect.

𝗧𝗮𝘀𝗸𝘀
𝗔𝗰𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗶𝘁𝘆 Teacher will send a short story. Students must read it and answer 5 questions.
𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺 Write a short summary of the story and explain the lesson.

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 Basic Internet Safety

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 𝗘𝘅𝗽𝗹𝗮𝗻𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻:

Internet safety is how to protect yourself and your personal information online. It prevents identity theft, cyberbullying, and other risks.

𝗣𝗮𝗿𝘁𝘀 𝗼𝗳 𝗜𝗻𝘁𝗲𝗿𝗻𝗲𝘁 𝗦𝗮𝗳𝗲𝘁𝘆:

  • 𝗣𝗮𝘀𝘀𝘄𝗼𝗿𝗱𝘀: Using strong, secure passwords.
  • 𝗣𝗲𝗿𝘀𝗼𝗻𝗮𝗹 𝗜𝗻𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗺𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻: Keeping your details private.
  • 𝗦𝘂𝘀𝗽𝗶𝗰𝗶𝗼𝘂𝘀 𝗟𝗶𝗻𝗸𝘀: Avoiding unsafe websites or messages.
  • 𝗣𝗿𝗶𝘃𝗮𝗰𝘆 𝗦𝗲𝘁𝘁𝗶𝗻𝗴𝘀: Adjusting social media or app settings.
  • 𝗦𝗮𝗳𝗲 𝗣𝗿𝗮𝗰𝘁𝗶𝗰𝗲𝘀: Logging out and verifying sources.

𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲:

  • Always create strong passwords
  • Do not share your personal address or contact info
  • Avoid clicking unknown links
  • Adjust your social media privacy settings
  • Log out of shared devices
𝗧𝗮𝘀𝗸𝘀
𝗔𝗰𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗶𝘁𝘆 Students will list 5 ways to stay safe on the internet and sent in the page.
𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺 Explain why protecting passwords and personal information is important.

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 Creative Thinking

𝗧𝗼𝗽𝗶𝗰 𝗘𝘅𝗽𝗹𝗮𝗻𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻:

Creative thinking is using imagination and original ideas to solve problems or express oneself. It allows students to plan goals and create innovative solutions.

𝗣𝗮𝗿𝘁𝘀 𝗼𝗳 𝗖𝗿𝗲𝗮𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲 𝗧𝗵𝗶𝗻𝗸𝗶𝗻𝗴:

  • 𝗜𝗱𝗲𝗮 𝗚𝗲𝗻𝗲𝗿𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻: Coming up with new concepts.
  • 𝗣𝗹𝗮𝗻𝗻𝗶𝗻𝗴: Organizing your thoughts.
  • 𝗩𝗶𝘀𝘂𝗮𝗹𝗶𝘇𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻: Representing ideas through drawing, writing, or other forms.
  • 𝗘𝘅𝗽𝗿𝗲𝘀𝘀𝗶𝗼𝗻: Showing ideas creatively.
  • 𝗘𝘃𝗮𝗹𝘂𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻: Reviewing and improving your ideas.

𝗘𝘅𝗮𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲:

Drawing a building representing your dream business.
- 𝗘𝘅𝗽𝗹𝗮𝗻𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻: Shows creativity, imagination, and determination to reach goals.

𝗧𝗮𝘀𝗸𝘀
